What a company is
A company is the primary entity in EMS. It acts as the employer and legal sponsor for the company track. Every expatriate in this track must belong to a company. A company can own multiple dormitories and have multiple expatriates.
1
Create the company record
Fill in the core fields: name, code (unique identifier used in imports), registration number, BRN, director name and NIC, incorporation date, industry, address, district, country, phone, and email.
The code field is the unique short identifier (e.g. "ACME01") — it appears in bulk imports and reports. Choose it carefully; it cannot be duplicated.
2
Add contacts
Add operational contacts for the company — HR manager, site coordinator, etc. Each contact has a name, position, phone, and email. These are informational only and do not become system users.
3
Add turnover records
Record the company's annual turnover figures by year and currency. These appear as a mini bar chart on the company detail page. Optional but useful for compliance reporting.
4
Upload company documents
Upload encrypted documents: business registration certificate, trade licence, MOA, etc. Set an expiry date on each. The system will alert you before they expire. If a document is renewed, upload the new version — the old one is archived automatically with a version history.
The admin panel defines which document types are mandatory. If any mandatory type is missing, a compliance badge appears on the company page and in the dashboard alerts.
5
Create dormitories (if housing expatriates)
If the company is responsible for housing, create one or more dormitories under the company. Each dormitory has a capacity (total beds), male/female lodger fields, contract dates, and permit dates. You can also do this later — before adding expatriates who need dormitory housing.
Dormitories belong to a company but expatriates from any company can technically be assigned to any dormitory. The company association is for organisational purposes.
6
Start a company checklist
Start a checklist for this company from one of the global checklist templates. The checklist tracks compliance tasks specific to the company (e.g. "Register with labour ministry", "Submit accommodation plan"). Items can be marked complete and notes added. Custom items can be added beyond the template.
7
Company is ready — add expatriates
The company is now fully set up. You can begin adding expatriates under it, assigning them to dormitories, and starting their onboarding workflows.
Dashboard compliance cards will flag the company if it has no dormitories, no checklist, or expatriates without onboarding records.
What an individual sponsor is
An individual sponsor is a private person (not a company) who sponsors one or more expatriates. The sponsored expatriates still belong to a company for employment purposes, but their legal sponsor and potentially their housing is this individual. This is a distinct sponsor track from the company track.
1
Create the individual sponsor record
Fill in: full name, NIC (unique — National Identity Card number), occupation, address, district, country, phone, mobile, and email.
The NIC is the unique key for individual sponsors. Attempting to create two sponsors with the same NIC returns a 422 error.
2
Add contacts
Add people to contact about this sponsor (a family member, lawyer, or representative). Same structure as company contacts: name, position, phone, email.
3
Upload sponsor documents
Upload the sponsor's identity documents, sponsorship licence, property ownership proof, etc. All encrypted with expiry tracking and version history. Mandatory document types configured in admin will show a compliance badge if missing.
4
Sponsor is ready — add expatriates
You can now create expatriates with this individual as their sponsor. Each expatriate still needs a company (employer), but their sponsor type will be INDIVIDUAL_SPONSOR and their housing can be WITH_SPONSOR (living at the sponsor's premises) or WITH_DORMITORY (in a registered dormitory).
A company must already exist before creating the expatriate — the company is the employer even when the sponsor is an individual.
Company track — what this means
Sponsor type: COMPANY Housing type: WITH_DORMITORY — The company is both employer and legal sponsor. The expatriate lives in a registered dormitory. This is the most common track.
1
Prerequisites: company + dormitory must exist
Before creating this expatriate you need: (a) the company record created and active, (b) at least one dormitory under that company with available capacity. Check the dormitory's occupancy — if it is at 100% capacity the assignment will be rejected.
2
Step 1 of form — personal info
Employee number (unique), first name, last name, nationality, date of birth, gender, phone, email, address in origin country, job title.
3
Step 2 — passport, visa, and work permit
Passport number, issue date, expiry date. Visa type and expiry date. Work permit number and expiry date. Provisional medical clearance number. All expiry dates are tracked — the system will alert you at 90, 30, and 7 days before each expires.
Leave no expiry date blank if you have the information. Missing expiry dates mean missing alerts — this is how renewals get missed.
4
Step 3 — sponsor and housing
Set Sponsor type: COMPANY — this auto-defaults housing type to WITH_DORMITORY. Select the company from the dropdown. Select the dormitory. The system checks that the dormitory has remaining capacity before saving.
Housing type WITH_SPONSOR is not available for the COMPANY sponsor track. Attempting to set it returns a 422 validation error.
5
Step 4 — emergency contacts
Add at least one emergency contact: name, relationship, phone number. These are separate from the expatriate's own contact details.
6
Save — dormitory counters update atomically
When the record is saved, the dormitory's male or female lodger counter is incremented automatically in the same database transaction. If the transaction fails, no counter changes — the count never drifts.
7
Upload photo and documents
Upload a profile photo (encrypted). Upload copies of the passport, visa, work permit, and medical clearance. Each document gets an expiry date and is tracked. Version history is maintained if documents are renewed and re-uploaded.
Uploading a document does not automatically fill in the expiry date on the main expatriate record. Update both — the document for the encrypted file, and the main record fields for alert tracking.
8
Start onboarding
From the Onboarding tab on the expatriate detail page, click "Start onboarding" and select a template. The steps are copied as a snapshot — editing the template later won't affect this record. Mark steps complete as they are done. Add notes to steps for context.
If the company has the global default onboarding template set, you can bulk-start onboarding for multiple expatriates at once from the onboarding records page.
Individual sponsor track — two housing options
Sponsor type: INDIVIDUAL_SPONSOR — The expatriate has an individual as their legal sponsor, not the company. They still have a company as their employer. Housing can be either WITH_SPONSOR (living at the sponsor's home/premises) or WITH_DORMITORY (in a registered dormitory, even though the sponsor is an individual).
1
Prerequisites: company AND individual sponsor must exist
You need both: (a) the company record (as employer) and (b) the individual sponsor record. If the expatriate will live in a dormitory, that dormitory must also exist with available capacity. If they will live with the sponsor, no dormitory is needed.
2
Steps 1 and 2 of form — same as company track
Personal info and passport/visa/permit details are identical to the company track. Fill in all expiry dates.
3
Step 3 — sponsor and housing (key differences)
Set Sponsor type: INDIVIDUAL_SPONSOR. The housing type field now offers both options: WITH_SPONSOR (default for this track) or WITH_DORMITORY.
WITH_SPONSOR: Select the individual sponsor from the dropdown. Leave dormitory blank. The expatriate's housing address is the sponsor's address. WITH_DORMITORY: Select the individual sponsor AND select a dormitory. The expatriate lives in the dormitory but is legally sponsored by the individual.
4
Save — behaviour depends on housing type
If WITH_DORMITORY: dormitory lodger counter increments atomically (same as company track). If WITH_SPONSOR: no dormitory counter changes. The expatriate appears in both the individual sponsor's expatriates list and the company's expatriates list.
5
Upload documents and start onboarding
Same process as the company track. Upload documents for both the expatriate and ensure the individual sponsor's documents are also current — the sponsor's document expiry is tracked separately and alerts are sent when their documents approach expiry.

How dormitories work
A dormitory is a registered housing facility. It belongs to a company (for organisational purposes) but can house expatriates from any company. Capacity is tracked in real time. Male and female lodger counts are maintained separately and updated atomically whenever an expatriate is assigned, unassigned, or transferred.

1
Create the dormitory under a company
Go to the company page → Dormitories tab → Add dormitory. Fill in: name, address, capacity (total beds), contract start/expiry dates, file number, permit number, permit start/expiry dates. The maleLodgers and femaleLodgers fields start at 0 and are managed by the system.
Do not manually edit maleLodgers or femaleLodgers in the database. They are managed transactionally by the application. Manual edits cause drift — use the admin reconciliation endpoint to fix counts if needed.
2
Upload dormitory documents
Upload the dormitory licence, health and safety certificate, fire safety certificate, etc. Set expiry dates. The system tracks permit expiry and contract expiry and sends alerts. Version history is maintained on all documents.
3
How lodger counts update — automatic, transactional
Every time an expatriate is created, updated, or transferred and their dormitory assignment or gender changes, the system runs these counter updates in the same database transaction as the main record change. Either both succeed or neither does — counts never drift due to partial saves.
Assign to dorm: +1 to maleLodgers or femaleLodgers based on gender
Remove from dorm: -1 from the matching counter
Transfer between dorms: -1 from old dorm, +1 in new dorm — all in one transaction
Gender update: -1 from old gender counter, +1 to new gender counter
4
Capacity validation on assignment
Before any expatriate is assigned or transferred to a dormitory, the system checks: current total lodgers (male + female) against capacity. If the assignment would exceed capacity, it is rejected with a validation error. The transfer approval step (MANAGER) also runs this check before executing.
5
Transferring an expatriate between dormitories
From the expatriate detail page → Transfer button. Select the new dormitory. The system creates a transfer record with status PENDING. A MANAGER must approve it. On approval, the old dormitory's counter decrements and the new one's increments — all in a single atomic transaction. The full transfer history (from, to, reason, date, who approved) is stored permanently.
Transfer records capture the complete before/after state: from company, to company, from dormitory, to dormitory, from sponsor type, to sponsor type. Even if the dormitory or company is later deleted, the transfer history remains intact.
6
Expiry alerts for dormitory contracts and permits
The system tracks contractExpiryDate and permitExpiryDate on every dormitory. At 90, 30, and 7 days before expiry (and on the expiry date itself) the daily cron job generates notifications. These appear in the notification bell and are emailed to users with EMS READ permission. The dashboard compliance card also flags dormitories whose contracts have expired.
7
Weekly reconciliation (recommended)
Run the admin reconciliation endpoint weekly: POST /api/admin/dormitories/reconcile. It recomputes all lodger counts from the actual active expatriate records and flags any discrepancies in the audit log. Any mismatch appears as a warning in the admin Jobs tab. This catches edge cases from failed transactions or data migrations.
